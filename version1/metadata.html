Each time you upload an image, you will give us the following data:

  • date and time of the moment you upload your photo
  • geographic coordinates (longitude and latitude) of your location at each image upload (we do not document your IP address)

   → in exchange you will see a satellite image (with the Google Earth Engine dataset) captured on the same day and time,
     from the same geo-location where you upload your image, with a radius of 10km bounding box;
   → in exchange you will see the satellite image of the land SCA-1 flies across at the same day and time

Each uploaded image will produce the following data:

  • each image will be assigned a unique content identifier (CID) as it is uploaded to and stored on IPFS;
  • text description of each uploaded image and the two satellite images, recognized and classified by image recognition models

   → in exchange you will know what land imagery SCA-1 hovers over at the same moment when you produce your image,
     and how far away you are from the SCA-1 satellite;
   → in exchange you will find out when the SCA-1 flies across any energy facilities (according to energy facility and climate change database)
     and how far away you are from such facility.



* All metadata will be displayed on this website viewable by the public for research purposes of this project;
  we do not conceal any other data or use any of the metadata for commercial, state-associated, or any other exchanges.
