SCA–1: Simulated Earthworks

Poetics and Language in Machine-Generated Landscapes

The satellite operates in Low Earth Orbit at approximately 450 km, aligning it with the altitude range of Earth imaging satellites typically used for cartography, environmental monitoring, and surveillance. Rather than acquiring data, however, this satellite functions as an emissive platform - displaying AI-generated landscapes that are not observational in nature, but instead constructed using artist-defined geographic coordinates and descriptive prompts.

Structured in three conceptual phases — remembered, reimagined, and erased — the work examines how representational regimes shift under conditions of artificial generation. This trajectory, from visual impression to algorithmic interpretation and eventual disappearance, mirrors the satellite’s operational timeline: a three-year orbital arc ending in atmospheric disintegration. The result is not a documentary image of the Earth, but a speculative terrain — generated by systems designed to simulate rather than record.

Language within the project functions less as narrative than as interface, shaping the parameters by which visual information is constructed and perceived. The satellite’s projections, viewed against the backdrop of the actual Earth, create a doubled image — one that foregrounds the mediation inherent in planetary computation and challenges the stability of representation itself. The work thus positions AI not as a tool of clarification, but as a generative agent in the reconfiguration of geographic space.
